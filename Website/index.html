What Do the Happiness Levels Mean?

This analysis contains data from three seperate data sources and attempts to identify major factors that may or may not influence the a country's happiness index rating. Explore the links below to examine the datasets more closely!

The World Happiness Index

The World Happiness Index gathers data from many sources and uses that data to quantify and measure the happiness of citizens in a country based on those metrics. This data shows metrics for the listed countries in 2015.

Suicide Rates 2015

This dataset measures suicide rates and related metrics in the year 2015 by country.

Gross National Income Data

This dataset displays gross national income by country for the year 2015. Wealth is often assocaited with happiness. Therefore, the wealth of a country will likely correlate with the happiness of its citizens.

Data Comparisons

This dataset compares gross national income, suicide rates, and the World Happiness Index for the year 2015. The metrics in each dataset contains information on major influencing factors which are likely to impact the happiness of the citizens in a country. Such as, the abundance of wealth within a country determines availability of food and other nessecities within society, which correlates with the happiness of its citizens.
